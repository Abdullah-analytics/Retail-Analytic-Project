Online Retail Data Analysis
Internship Project – Zaalima Development
📌 Description
This project analyzes sales data of an online retail store from 2009 to 2011 to uncover trends, understand customer behavior, and generate actionable insights.
The primary focus is on using PostgreSQL for data extraction, transformation, and advanced analytical queries.
Power BI is used to build interactive dashboards and visualize key business metrics.
Python is used selectively for lightweight data cleaning and RFM preparation.
📌 Project Overview
This project examines three years (2009–2011) of online retail transaction data to evaluate revenue performance, product trends, customer segmentation, churn patterns, and geographic contribution.
The analysis transforms raw transactional records into structured insights that support data-driven decision-making.
🏢 Business Context
The business operates as an online retailer selling low-cost, high-volume gift items to wholesalers, retailers, boutiques, and individual consumers across multiple countries.
Orders are recorded as invoices.
Cancellations are represented using negative quantities or invoice numbers starting with “C”.

📈 Executive Summary of Findings
Revenue increased significantly in 2010
Revenue declined in 2011
The UK contributes the majority of total revenue
Sales are concentrated among a limited number of customers and products
Customer churn increased over time
Cancellation rates rose in later years
Strong seasonal peaks occur in November and December
