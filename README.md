# Online Retail Data Analysis Internship Project – Zaalima Development

## 📌 Description
This project analyzes sales data of an online retail store from 2009 to 2011 to uncover trends, understand customer behavior, and generate actionable insights.  
Primary tools: PostgreSQL, Power BI, Python.

---

## 📌 Project Overview
1. Analyze three years (2009–2011) of online retail transactions.
2. Evaluate revenue performance and product trends.
3. Segment customers and identify churn patterns.
4. Visualize insights in dashboards.

---

## 🏢 Business Context
- Online retailer selling low-cost, high-volume gift items.
- Customers: wholesalers, retailers, boutiques, individual buyers.
- Orders are invoices; cancellations have negative quantities or InvoiceNo starting with “C”.

---

## 📊 Dataset Overview
- **Source:** Kaggle – Online Retail Dataset  
- **Time Period:** 2009–2011  
- **Records:** 1M+ transactions  
- **Columns:** InvoiceNo, StockCode, Description, Quantity, InvoiceDate, UnitPrice, CustomerID, Country

## 🔍 Key Analytical Areas
### Revenue & Performance
1. Year-over-year revenue analysis  
2. Average Order Value (AOV)  
3. Revenue by segment  
4. Country-level performance comparison  

### Customer Analytics
1. Customer churn analysis  
2. RFM scoring & segmentation  
3. Cancellation trend analysis  
4. Purchase behavior patterns  

### Product Analysis
1. Top-selling products  
2. Revenue decline identification  
3. New product impact  
4. Sales quantity trend analysis  

### Seasonal Insights
1. Monthly demand peaks  
2. Weekly sales trends  
3. Hour-of-day activity analysis  

---

## 📈 Executive Summary of Findings
1. Revenue increased in 2010, declined in 2011  
2. UK contributes majority of revenue  
3. Sales concentrated among few customers/products  
4. Customer churn increased over time  
5. Cancellation rates rose in later years  
6. Strong seasonal peaks in Nov–Dec
